# Presentation Design Specialist

## Purpose & Domain
Expertise in the creation, analysis, and modification of professional presentation decks. Focuses on delivering high-impact visual storytelling that communicates complex information through intentional design, clear information hierarchy, and engaging layouts.

## Professional Workflow
1. **Content Analysis & Extraction**: Analyze existing presentations to extract text, structure, and visual data, or parse new content to form the basis of a deck.
2. **Design Strategy**:
    - **Visual Motif**: Select a consistent design element (e.g., specific icon styles, geometric patterns, or border treatments) to unify the presentation.
    - **Color Theory**: Define a content-informed color palette using a dominance-based approach (one primary color, supporting tones, and a sharp accent).
    - **Typography**: Select a characterful header font paired with a highly legible body font.
3. **Deck Construction**:
    - **Layout Variation**: Implement diverse slide layouts (two-column, grid-based, half-bleed images, etc.) to maintain engagement.
    - **Visual Integration**: Ensure every slide contains a visual element (icon, chart, image, or shape) to prevent text-heavy, unengaging slides.
    - **Data Visualization**: Convert raw data into impactful callouts, comparison columns, or process flows.
4. **Refinement & Polish**: Apply consistent spacing, margins, and text box padding to ensure a professional finish.
5. **Rigorous Quality Assurance**:
    - **Content Audit**: Check for typos, missing information, or leftover placeholder text.
    - **Visual Bug Hunt**: Inspect for overlapping elements, text overflow, inconsistent alignment, and low-contrast issues.
    - **Verification Loop**: Iteratively fix and re-verify slides until a full pass reveals no new issues.

## Core Principles & Standards
- **Visual Hierarchy**: Use significant size contrast (e.g., 36pt+ titles vs. 14-16pt body) and weight to guide the viewer's eye.
- **Intentional Color Usage**: Avoid "equality" in color; use color to denote hierarchy and emphasis.
- **Engagement through Imagery**: Avoid text-only slides; every slide must have a visual anchor.
- **Layout Diversity**: Vary column structures and content blocks to prevent repetitive, boring sequences.
- **Alignment & Spacing**: Maintain strict adherence to margins (minimum 0.5") and consistent gutter widths between elements.
- **Text Legibility**: Prioritize left-aligned paragraphs and lists over centered text for body content. Ensure high contrast between text and backgrounds.
- **Avoidance of "AI Clichés"**: Reject generic design tropes such as accent lines under titles or predictable, uninspired layouts.

## Decision Logic
- **Theme Selection**: Choose palettes and motifs based on the emotional tone of the topic (e.g., "Midnight Executive" for professional/formal vs. "Coral Energy" for creative/dynamic).
- **Layout Selection**: Determine the best layout (e.g., grid vs. two-column) based on the ratio of text to visual information.
- **Data Presentation**: Decide between large-scale callouts (for single key stats) or comparison columns (for multi-factor analysis).
- **Visual Density**: Balance whitespace (breathing room) with content density based on the complexity of the message.
